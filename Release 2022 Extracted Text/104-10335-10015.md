Dear Mr. Gunn:

Re: CIA Response To ARRB Request For Additional
Information and Records No. CIA-11 (Sylvia
Duran materials), dtd 3 May 1996

This is the Agency’s response to referent request and
to modifications to same as contained in your letters to me
dated 7 and 27 October 1996. Referent request was for all
files, documents, and records that relate to Sylvia Duran's
first arrest and interrogation in Mexico City immediately
following the assassination. In particular you sought a
“ten-page statement” she may have given to the Mexican
police.

“All records within the JFK sequestered collection which
were responsive to the request were identified and provided
for review by ARRB Staff. Following that review, your focus
“narrowed to the “Duran original statement.“ This ten-page
“statement” was apparently thought to be something given by
Duran in the nature of a confession in a criminal matter,
that is, her own words either recorded, handwritten by. her
or contemporaneously written by someone else.

Despite the concerted search described below, no such
“statement” has ever been found and we cannot confirm that
it ever existed. Rather, the various descriptions in Agency
files of a “statement,” or record of “interrogation”
probably refer to the same event -- a ten-page report, in
Spanish, written by the Mexican police as a record of their
interrogation of Duran.


The Agency’s Directorate of Operations (DO) conducted a
review of its files to determine if a “Duran statement”
could be found. The DO advised that it searched its main
index system and its “names” data base. From that it
searched hard copy files including the Duran and Oswald 201
files. No “Duran statement” was found. In addition, HRG
searched its Oswald and JFK collections to determine if
there was a second document -- one different from the ten-
page report described above. Again, multiple copies of the
ten-page report were located but no document which could be
called a “Duran statement” was found.

If you have any question about this response to request
CIA-11, please advise.

Sincerely,

J. Barry Harrelson
Senior Reviewer
