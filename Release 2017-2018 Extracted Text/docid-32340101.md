May 29, 1968

ITICE

Information has been received that MICHAEL MCLANEY,

who has been identified as having had an interest in Bahamian

gambling matters, and who has been reported in the press as

having been in an advisory capacity on these matters to ,

Premier LYNDEN O. PINDLING of the Bahama Islands, has been

told by an unidentified source that an attempt may be made

to injure Premier PINDLING.

This individual advised MCLANEY

that "racket individuals" are dissatisfied with the current

restrictions placed on casino gambling in the Bahamas and

hold PINDLING responsible for these restrictions.

The unidentified source who contacted McLANEY

was said to have been drinking,

but was not identified as

being intoxicated.

The information above was furnished telephonically

Assistant Commissioner STANLEY R$ MOIR, Bahamas Police

Force, Nassau.

MCLANEY also furnished same to JAMES SHEPARD,

a Bahamian representative who is on a trip with PINDLING,

and to ALBERT A. FOULKES, a member of House of Assembly.

The above information relative to Premier PINDLING

is being furnished for your information.
